02 — About

I like tools that disappear into someone's workday.

The best feedback I get isn't "this looks great" — it's that someone stopped thinking about the software at all and just got their invoicing done.

How I work

I usually start with the paper trail — the notebook, the WhatsApp messages, the spreadsheet someone built five years ago and never stopped patching. That's where the real requirements live. From there I build something narrow enough to actually finish and sturdy enough to survive daily use: stock counts that don't drift, invoices that calculate tax correctly the first time, and dashboards that answer the question someone actually asked.

Skills & tools

  • HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript — including single-file applications built for low-friction deployment
  • Front-end UI work: dashboards, data tables, animated interfaces, canvas-rendered charts
  • Business logic: stock/inventory tracking, GST-compliant invoicing, scheduling and notifications
  • Accessibility-minded markup and form design

What I care about

Clarity over cleverness
If a shop owner can't explain how a feature works after one demo, it's too complicated.
Data that holds up
Stock counts and invoice totals have to be right every time, not most of the time.
Software that respects attention
Interfaces should be quick to use and easy to come back to after a busy week.
